Golden Retriever

The ever-popular Golden Retriever is intelligent, friendly, and incredibly loyal.

Quick Facts

  • Size: Large
  • Lifespan: 10–12 Years
  • Temperament: Friendly, Loyal, Smart
  • Energy: Moderate
  • Shedding: High
  • Breed Group: Sporting

Personality & Training

  • Tendency to Bark: High
  • Trainability: High

Good For:

  • Kids
  • Cats
  • Other Dogs
  • Large Living Spaces
  • Seniors
  • First-Time Owners
  • Cold Climates
  • Water Activities

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: F: 22–23 in, M: 23–24 in
  • Weight: F: 55–65 lbs, M: 65–75 lbs
  • Grooming: Weekly brushing
  • Drool: Low
  • Colors: Dark Golden, Cream, Light Golden
  • Markings: White, Black, None
  • Varieties: American, English

Golden Retriever Health & Care

Essential care tips to keep your Golden healthy and happy.

Grooming & Maintenance

  • Weekly coat brushing; daily during shedding seasons
  • Regular ear cleaning
  • Dental brushing several times a week
  • Monthly nail trimming

Exercise & Diet

  • Daily walks and active playtime
  • Balanced diet suited to age and activity level
  • Weight monitoring
  • Constant fresh water access

Common Health Issues

  • Hip & elbow dysplasia
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Skin allergies & hotspots
  • Increased cancer risks

Preventative Care

  • Annual vet check-ups
  • Updated vaccinations
  • Genetic testing
  • Consistent routine care

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Golden Retrievers good family dogs?

Yes, they’re friendly, patient, and love children. Great for family life.

How much exercise do they need?

At least one hour daily: walking, running, or swimming.

Do they shed a lot?

Yes, especially during seasonal changes. Brush often!

Are they easy to train?

Absolutely. They’re smart and eager to please.

What’s their lifespan?

Typically 10–12 years with good health care.

Are Golden Retrievers good first dogs?

The Golden Retriever are highly sociable temperament and affectionate personality make them a great decision for first-time dog owners, and dog owners with children.

Do Golden Retrievers bite?

Any dog can bite. However, Golden Retrievers are exceptionally gentle and rarely show aggression. Though, like all dogs regular training and socialization are essential to ensuring your Golden Retriever learns appropriate behavior.
